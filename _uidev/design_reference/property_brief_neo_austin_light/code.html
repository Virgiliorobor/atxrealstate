The Narrative.

A monolithic intervention in the heart of Bouldin Creek. This residence is not merely constructed; it is instantiated. Rejecting the vernacular of faux-farmhouse gentrification, Project 04 presents an uncompromising facade of board-formed concrete and structural steel, a brutalist anchor point in an evolving neighborhood.

The interior volume is defined by aggressive, light-filled voids. A central atrium, spanning three stories, organizes the programmatic spaces, ensuring that every living zone is flooded with indirect northern light. The material palette is intentionally austere: polished concrete floors, blackened steel casework, and expansive planes of white gallery-grade drywall designed to showcase large-format contemporary art.

"It forces a pause. It is a machine for living that demands your full attention."

Technological integration is seamless but subordinate to the architecture. Climate control, illumination, and security are managed via an invisible, locally-hosted neural network, completely decoupled from external cloud dependencies. This is a fortress of privacy, an analog sanctuary with a digital nervous system.
