## ⚠ IMPORTANT NOTICE — WIRE FRAUD ALERT

Real estate transactions are a primary target for wire fraud. Criminals intercept email communications and send fraudulent wire instructions that appear to come from your agent, escrow officer, or title company.

**This firm will NEVER send you wire instructions by email or text.**

---

### HOW WIRE FRAUD WORKS

1. A criminal monitors email communications related to your transaction
2. They send you an email that appears to be from your agent or title company
3. The email contains wiring instructions to a fraudulent account
4. Once funds are wired, they are nearly impossible to recover

---

### HOW TO PROTECT YOURSELF

**BEFORE wiring any funds, you must:**

- [ ] Call {{title_company}} directly at a phone number you found independently — **not** a number provided in an email
- [ ] Verbally confirm the wire instructions with a live person
- [ ] Verify the account name matches the title company exactly
- [ ] Call your agent {{agent_name}} at {{agent_phone}} to confirm if anything seems unusual

**NEVER:**
- Wire funds based solely on emailed instructions
- Click links in emails claiming to update wire instructions
- Assume an email is legitimate because it looks official

---

### IF YOU SUSPECT FRAUD

Contact immediately:
- Your bank — request a wire recall
- Your agent: {{agent_name}} at {{agent_phone}}
- The FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center: ic3.gov
- The Texas Real Estate Commission: trec.texas.gov
