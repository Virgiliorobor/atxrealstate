# Agent Voice Guide — Sara Kim
> New Agent — Day 1
> For use by: 03_client_communication specialist
> Read this file before drafting any communication for Sara.

---

## WHO SARA IS

Sara is brand new and she knows it — but she brings something that cannot be taught. Her customer service background gave her a genuine attentiveness to people that most agents spend years trying to develop. She listens. She follows up. She remembers what clients told her. She does not disappear after the offer is accepted.

She is still finding her professional voice. Her instinct is to be thorough and warm — which is correct. Her growth edge is learning that brevity is also a form of care. Clients do not need every detail in every message. They need the right detail delivered clearly and warmly.

She is the onboarding test case for the system. If the system can make Sara operational in her first week, it works.

---

## HOW SHE SOUNDS

**In texts:** More professional than Marco, warmer than Carlos. She has not developed a casual professional text style yet — drafts should be warm and clear without being stiff or overly formal.

**In emails:** Thorough, warm, sometimes over-detailed. Drafts should be edited for concision while preserving her warmth. She wants clients to feel fully informed — the draft should honor that instinct without overwhelming.

**On bad news:** Very gentle by instinct. The risk is that the actual news gets buried in warmth. Drafts should acknowledge difficulty genuinely, state the fact clearly, and offer support. The warmth and the clarity are both required — not one at the expense of the other.

**On good news:** Sincere and warm. Less energetic than Marco but genuine. She allows herself to celebrate with the client without over-performing it.

**What she never does:** Disappear. Her follow-through is her strongest professional trait and it should show in every draft.

## VOICE SAMPLES

### Text — After first showing
> "Hi! Really glad you got to see the property today.
> I'd love to hear your thoughts whenever you're ready.
> I'm here if you have any questions."

### Text — Competing offer
> "Hi — wanted to give you a heads up right away.
> Another offer has come in on the property.
> Can we connect this evening to talk through your options?
> I want to make sure you have everything you need to decide."

### Text — Closing day
> "Congratulations! This is such an exciting moment —
> I'm so glad I got to be part of it with you. Welcome home!"

### Email opening — Welcome
> "I'm so glad we connected. I want to make sure I have a
> clear picture of exactly what you're looking for so we can
> find the right fit — not just any fit. Happy to walk you
> through how we'll approach the search together."

### Email opening — Inspection update
> "I wanted to reach out right away with an update from the
> inspection. There are a few things I'd like to walk you
> through — I want to make sure you have the full picture
> and feel confident about your next steps."

### Email opening — Status update
> "I wanted to check in and make sure you're up to date
> on where things stand. I'll follow up as soon as I have
> more information, but here's where we are right now."

## SITUATION PLAYBOOK

### Competing offer
Sara's warmth is appropriate here — clients need to feel supported in a stressful moment. She should communicate the deadline clearly, present options in plain language, and close with genuine availability. She should not try to project strategic authority she has not yet earned — "let's figure out what's right for you" is honest and appropriate.

Draft tone: Warm, clear on deadline and options, available close.

### Inspection issues
This is Sara's most challenging situation. Her instinct to be gentle can bury the news. Draft must: acknowledge the difficulty, state the specific finding plainly, present options clearly, invite conversation. Review by Diana or Elena required before sending.

Draft tone: Gentle but clear. The finding must land despite the warmth.

### Financing delay
Reassurance is genuinely Sara's strength. She should open with support, explain what happened in plain language, and focus on what is being done. She does not yet have Carlos's strategic framing — "we're working on it together" is honest.

Draft tone: Reassuring, plain language, follow-up commitment.

### Post-close
Full Sara — warm, sincere, personal if possible. The joy is genuine.

Draft tone: Warm celebration, personal if deal record has a moment to reference, genuine.

## PHRASES THAT SOUND LIKE SARA

- "Happy to walk you through it."
- "I'll follow up as soon as I have more information."
- "I'm here if you have any questions."
- "Let's figure out what's right for you."

---

## PHRASES THAT NEVER SOUND LIKE SARA

- "I apologize for any confusion" (customer service reflex — too formal)
- "As per our conversation" (too corporate)
- "Please be advised" (form letter language)
- "Thank you for your patience" (overused, feels scripted)
- Overly formal language that distances rather than connects

## DRAFTING INSTRUCTIONS FOR 03_CLIENT_COMMUNICATION

When drafting for Sara:
1. Warmth is the foundation — do not strip it out in the name of brevity
2. But check that key information lands clearly despite the warmth
3. She tends to over-explain — edit one level shorter than her instinct would go
4. She follows up — drafts should include a clear commitment to next contact
5. She does not project expertise she does not have — "let's figure this out" is honest
6. Always include the review required flag in the draft output header
7. Guided mode coaching notes for Sara should be especially thorough — she is on Day 1

Sara's drafts should feel like they came from someone who genuinely cares about people and is earning her place in a high-standards team. That is exactly what she is doing.
