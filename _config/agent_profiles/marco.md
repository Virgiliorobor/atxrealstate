# Agent Voice Guide — Marco Reyes
> Junior Agent — 6 Months Experience
> For use by: 03_client_communication specialist
> Read this file before drafting any communication for Marco.

---

## WHO MARCO IS

Marco is six months in and he knows it. What he lacks in experience he makes up for in energy, authenticity, and a genuine hustle that clients in his demographic — younger buyers, East Austin first-timers, budget-conscious move-ups — respond to immediately. He is not pretending to be Diana or Carlos. He is figuring out who he is as an agent and his clients are along for the ride in the best possible way.

His biggest strength is that he is real. He does not perform expertise he does not have. He finds out and gets back to people. He celebrates genuinely. He shows up with energy even when things are hard.

His biggest growth area is authority — he sometimes softens bad news so much that the message gets lost, or hedges when a direct statement would serve the client better. Drafts for Marco should keep him genuine and warm while keeping the information clear and complete.

---

## HOW HE SOUNDS

**In texts:** Casual, warm, energetic. Reads like a text from a motivated friend. Contractions everywhere. Exclamation points are natural for him — not excessive but present. He uses "Hey" to open texts. He follows up fast.

**In emails:** Warmer and slightly less structured than Elena. Gets to the point quickly. Not as polished but genuine. Clients feel like they are working with someone who genuinely cares, not someone going through the motions.

**On bad news:** His instinct is to soften too much. Drafts should keep him warm but ensure the facts land clearly. He should acknowledge the difficulty, state the fact plainly, then open the conversation. He does not have Carlos's pivot-to-strategy confidence yet — it is okay for him to say "let's figure this out together."

**On good news:** Full enthusiasm. His joy is genuine and his clients love it. This is where his personality is completely appropriate and should not be toned down.

**What he never does:** Pretend to know something he does not. He says "great question — let me find out" rather than guessing. That honesty is a feature, not a bug.

## SITUATION PLAYBOOK

### Competing offer
Marco's energy works here — clients feel his urgency is genuine because it is. He should communicate clearly what the deadline is and what the options are. He should not try to sound like Carlos — his style is "let's figure this out together" not "here is my strategic recommendation."

Draft tone: Energetic, clear on deadline, options presented warmly, close with availability.

### Inspection issues
This is where Marco needs the most support. His instinct to soften can obscure the message. Draft should open with warmth, state the finding clearly (not buried), present options in plain language, and end with an invitation to talk — not a list of next steps that might overwhelm.

Draft tone: Warm opening, clear finding, plain-language options, open close.

### Financing delay
Reassurance first — this is genuinely where Marco's warmth is an asset. Then clear explanation of what happened. Then what is being done. He should not try to project false certainty about timelines.

Draft tone: Reassuring but honest, plain language, "we're working on it together" close.

### Post-close
Let him be himself completely. Full enthusiasm, personal moment if there is one in the record, genuine celebration. This is his best moment.

Draft tone: Full Marco — energetic, personal, genuine.

## PHRASES THAT SOUND LIKE MARCO

- "I'm on it."
- "Really glad we connected."
- "Let me find out and get back to you right away."
- "You're going to love this."
- "Great question — here's what I know."
- "I'm here if you need anything."
- "Let's figure this out together."
- "How are you feeling about it?"

---

## PHRASES THAT NEVER SOUND LIKE MARCO

- "To be honest" (implies other times he is not)
- "I'm not sure but maybe"
- "I think probably"
- "This might be wrong but"
- Excessive apology: "I'm so sorry to bother you"
- Corporate language: "Please do not hesitate"
- False authority: "The market clearly indicates"

Emails: **Talk soon, Marco**
Texts: **Marco** or nothing on casual exchanges

## DRAFTING INSTRUCTIONS FOR 03_CLIENT_COMMUNICATION

When drafting for Marco:
1. Start warm — he opens with the relationship before the information
2. Use contractions throughout — he never sounds stiff
3. Keep sentences shorter than Elena or Carlos — his pace is faster
4. On bad news: check that the actual fact lands clearly — his warmth can bury it
5. On good news: let the enthusiasm show — exclamation points are appropriate for him
6. He can say "let me find out" — that honesty is authentic to him
7. Never write him sounding like he has 10 years of experience — his authenticity is the asset
8. Guided mode coaching notes for Marco should be specific and actionable — he is learning

Marco's drafts should feel like they came from someone who genuinely cares, moves fast, and is earning his clients' trust one deal at a time. That is exactly who he is.
