## AUSTIN MSA — MARKET OVERVIEW (March 2026)

### Market Condition Summary
The Austin market has entered a normalized phase following the 2021-2024 volatility period. Supply and demand are returning to historical equilibrium. This is a buyer's market with meaningful negotiating leverage — but affordability remains challenged by mortgage rates stabilized at 6.5%–7%.

### Key Performance Indicators

| Metric | March 2026 | YoY Change | What It Means |
|---|---|---|---|
| Median Sales Price | $426,220 | -3.0% | Stabilization post-peak |
| Active Listings | 10,867 | -8.9% | Inventory absorbing |
| Months of Inventory | 5.5 months | +0.8 months | Balanced market reached |
| Average Days on Market | 85 days | +5.0 days | Normalized sales cycle |
| Pending Sales | 3,557 | +15.4% | Buyer demand resurgent |
| Median Rental Price | $2,000 | -7.0% | Rental sector relief |
| Sale-to-List Ratio | 92.8% | -1.2% | Buyers negotiating successfully |

### Peak vs. Current Context
- All-time peak: $550,000 (May 2022)
- Current median: $426,220 — approximately 24.55% below peak
- Projected return to peak: September 2032 (based on 4.458% annual appreciation)
- Current environment: Buyer's window — 18-20% below peak pricing available

### Rental Market
- Median rental: $2,000/mo (-7.0% YoY)
- Closed leases volume: +10.5% — demand strong but buyers waiting for rate stability
- Rental inventory: 1.9 months — tight, competitive

### Strategic Outlook (2026-2027)
- Market bottom projected: Q3 2026
- Post-bottom appreciation: 3-4% annually beginning 2027
- Core themes for buyers: patience and leverage
- Core themes for sellers: pricing discipline — overpriced listings fail at 53%+ rate in premium zones
