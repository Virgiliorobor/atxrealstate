## NEIGHBORHOOD IDENTITY

**Character:** Stable, family-focused, old-growth Austin feel. Mid-century ranch homes on large lots. Some of the city's highest-rated public schools. Anchored by the Domain Effect — Austin's premier tech and retail hub proximity drives sustained demand.

**Location:** North Austin, approximately 6–8 miles from downtown. Adjacent to the Domain/tech corridor.

**Sub-neighborhoods:**
- **Allandale** — Most established, highest median price, low inventory, teardown-to-rebuild cycle active
- **Crestview** — Slightly more affordable, excellent transit access, fastest-clearing inventory in 2026
- **78758 (broader North Austin)** — Best entry price point in the zone, more suburban character

**Lifestyle:** Burnet Road corridor — dining, coffee, everyday conveniences. Car-dependent for most residents but Crestview Metrorail provides downtown access. Violet Crown Trail for cyclists.

**No mandatory HOAs in Allandale** — flexibility for renovations and ADU additions.

### Key Market Dynamics

**Allandale:** Exceptional resilience — only zone showing price appreciation in 2026. Low inventory (20 listings). Active teardown-to-rebuild cycle: original ranches purchased at $600k–$700k renovated or replaced and resold at $900k–$1.1M. 83 closed sales in trailing 12 months.

**Crestview:** Price down 15.3% but sales volume up 125% — a classic "clearing" pattern. Inventory is finding its market price. Buyers who move now are capturing value before volume stabilizes prices.

**78758:** Best dollar-per-square-foot in the zone. More suburban character, further from Burnet Road amenities, but strong school access and growing tech employer proximity.

## ENVIRONMENTAL RISKS

North Austin faces serious long-term climate risks that are beginning to affect insurance costs and buyer sentiment. Must be raised with clients:

**Heat:** 100% of properties face severe heat risk over next 30 years. Projected 214% increase in days over 108°F. Insurance and cooling costs are a real long-term consideration.

**Wind:** 92% of properties face severe wind risk from hurricane events.

**Flood:** Minimal risk (2%)
**Fire:** Minimal risk (1%)

**Agent protocol:** Heat and wind risk data should be disclosed proactively. Insurance premiums in this zone have been rising. Buyers should be advised to get insurance quotes early in the process.

## STRATEGIC NOTES FOR DIANA'S TEAM

**Buyer strategy at $500k–$750k:**
Crestview is the sweet spot. Prices down 15.3%, volume up 125% — this is a market actively clearing to fair value. Move now before the volume normalizes prices upward. Hardy Dr and Morrow St comps show 3/2 options available in this range.

**Buyer strategy at $750k–$1M+:**
Allandale is showing appreciation (+3.3%) while every other zone corrects — this is the most resilient sub-market in Diana's coverage area. Low inventory (20 listings) with active teardown premium. Buyers here are competing against renovation flippers and long-term hold investors.

**Buyer strategy at $400k–$500k:**
78758 delivers the most square footage per dollar in the zone. Less walkable, more suburban, but school access and tech proximity are strong. Best entry point for price-sensitive buyers who need space.

**Seller strategy:**
Domain Effect and school quality are the two primary narratives. Allandale sellers have pricing power right now — rare in this market. Crestview sellers need to price to the clearing market, not to aspirational 2022 values.

**Strategic recommendation (per research):**
Target Allandale for long-term equity growth. The teardown premium and school zone combination is the strongest appreciation story in a 5–10 year hold scenario.
