## NEIGHBORHOOD IDENTITY

**Character:** Austin's urban evolution engine. Transitioned from speculative gentrification frontier to mature urban core. Defined by art, music, food culture, and proximity to downtown. Strong buyer interest maintained despite broader market cooling.

**Location:** East of downtown Austin. 1.5 miles to city center. Lady Bird Lake and Rainey Street district to the south.

**Lifestyle:** High walkability in Holly and Cherrywood corridors. Vibrant social scene. Mix of long-term residents and young professional transplants.

**Key Sub-Neighborhoods:**
- **Holly** — modern luxury infill, rooftop decks, skyline views, proximity to Lady Bird Lake
- **Cherrywood** — historic 1930s–1950s cottages, tree-lined streets, UT Austin proximity, quieter and more established
- **Mueller** — new urbanist master-planned, highest valuations in the zone
- **Govalle** — emerging, investor focus, most affordable entry point

## MAJOR INFRASTRUCTURE PROJECTS (2026) — HIGH VALUE FOR CLIENT CONVERSATIONS

**I-35 Capital Express Central**
Lowering the highway's main lanes between Lady Bird Lake and Airport Boulevard. Removes the physical barrier between East and West Austin. Creates up to 25 acres of new public space. Expected to significantly boost long-term property values in Holly and Cherrywood. Timeline: multi-year project underway.

**Project Connect**
Austin's transit system expansion including new light rail downtown stops. Enhances East Austin connectivity to the metro. Crestview Metrorail Station already operational.

**Pathways at Rosewood Courts**
Redevelopment of historic public housing site. Deeply affordable multifamily and townhomes. Preserves eight original buildings honoring Juneteenth and civil rights history.

**Holly Missing Middle Housing**
Pilot high-density affordable housing project at North Pleasant Valley Road and East 5th Street. Designed to respect historic bungalow fabric scale.

## STRATEGIC NOTES FOR DIANA'S TEAM

**Buyer strategy at $400k–$650k (Chen profile range):**
Cherrywood is the realistic zone at this budget. Current market is favorable — prices down 12.4% and DOM at 191 days means negotiating power. Buyers should not rush — this sub-market rewards patience and due diligence. Govalle offers more space per dollar but with more uncertainty.

**Buyer strategy at $650k–$900k:**
Holly offers the lifestyle premium — Lady Bird Lake proximity, modern homes, walkable social scene. Expect less negotiating room here as this segment is more active.

**Seller strategy:**
I-35 project is the strongest long-term value narrative available. Price correctly — DOM data shows buyers are not rewarding overpriced listings with speed. Professional marketing emphasizing neighborhood evolution and infrastructure investment is key.

**Long-term hold argument:**
East Austin is the highest-growth narrative in the city. I-35 project alone could add significant value to properties in its immediate footprint. For buyers with 5–10 year horizon this is the strongest appreciation story.
